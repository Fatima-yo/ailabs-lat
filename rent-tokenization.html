Rent Tokenization

Blockchain-Backed Fractional Real Estate Investment

Own a piece. Earn the rent. Trust the contract.
01 Problem
  • Real estate investment requires high minimum capital and private relationships
  • Cross-border entry is blocked by local legal complexity
  • Rental-income streams are inaccessible to retail investors
  • No transparent, liquid secondary market for real estate positions
02 Solution

A regulated marketplace where real estate developers list projects and investors acquire fractional economic interests through blockchain-based tokens.

Each token represents a clearly documented legal right — SPV equity, trust interest, or contractual claim — not vague crypto exposure. The platform is a regulated real-world asset marketplace first, and a crypto application second.

Two layers:

  • Legal layer — contracts, ownership structures, investor rights, issuer obligations, disclosures, compliance controls, and transfer restrictions
  • Token layer — blockchain-based representation of rights, distribution accounting, investor registry, secondary transfer controls, and governance workflows
Core Principle Legal clarity + blockchain transparency
03 Legal Structure

Each listed project is held in a project-specific SPV. Investors acquire tokenized economic interests in that SPV — or a legally documented contractual claim against it.

Example asset type — rental real estate development project

Investor value proposition:

  • Fractional ownership or economic participation
  • Rental-income distributions
  • Exposure to real estate appreciation
  • Transparent project reporting
  • On-chain registry and distribution history
  • Potential secondary liquidity

Developer value proposition:

  • Broader investor base and faster subscription
  • Automated compliance gates
  • Transparent cap table and investor ledger
  • Tokenized distribution workflows
Key Design Rule Legal register is authoritative — blockchain is the transparency layer

05 Product Experience — MVP

A controlled, legally conservative pilot: one project, verified investors, restricted tokens, and clear legal documentation throughout.

Investor flow

  1. Create account and complete KYC
  2. Pass eligibility assessment
  3. Review project documentation and risk disclosures
  4. Execute subscription agreement
  5. Receive permissioned tokens after approval
  6. Track distributions and holding history on dashboard

Developer flow

  1. Complete KYB verification
  2. Submit project documents and financial disclosures
  3. Pass due diligence workflow
  4. List project and raise capital
  5. Upload rent income for distribution calculation
06 Risks & Mitigation
  • Legal / regulatory: assume securities regulation applies; use compliant offering exemptions; restrict transfers; maintain eligibility checks
  • Ownership mismatch: clearly define token rights; show legal structure on each listing; require investor acknowledgement
  • Liquidity: disclose limits upfront; provide controlled secondary process; offer buyout voting; avoid guaranteed liquidity language
  • Smart contract: audited contracts; multisig on all privileged actions; timelocks for upgrades; full audit trail of deployments and changes
  • Compliance: whitelisted wallets only; KYT monitoring; on-chain transfer checks; freeze powers where legally required
